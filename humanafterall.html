Human After All

14th of March, 2005


Human After All marked a significant departure from the duo's previous ventures. It deviated from the shiny, sample-studded catchy melodies that had become synonymous with the Daft Punk name. Instead, they embraced simplicity, repetition, and, at times, an almost barbaric quality in the soundscapes present across its track list.


However, this departure was far from a mistake. The pair felt drawn to the idea of creating the antithesis of what Discovery represented. They traded a highly polished, sparkly, and clean atmosphere for distorted electric guitars and warped vocals, crafting a musical equivalent of a handcrafted brutalist stone axe. This rugged and raw aesthetic echoed the themes of the album and its messages.


While Human After All didn't reach the same levels of critical and commercial success as their two previous albums, it showcased the duo's willingness to take risks and explore new sounds while remaining true to the core vision behind their music.
